Here it is, fully plugged in—clean, no gaps:

⸻

1. Relationship Flow (core system)

Request sent → system message → accept → linked → messaging opens → assign to property → work begins
	•	Before acceptance: no messaging, no access
	•	After acceptance: connected, messaging only
	•	After assignment: can see and work on that property

⸻

2. Invite Messages (role-based, consistent)
	•	Homeowner → Trade Pro
“Hi, this is Sheila. Let’s team up to work on my home.”
	•	Trade Pro → Homeowner
“Hi, this is [Name]. Let’s team up to work on and improve your home.”
	•	Facility → Trade Pro / Vendor
“Hi, this is [Company]. Let’s team up to maintain our properties.”
	•	Trade Pro → Service Provider
“Hi, this is [Name]. Let’s team up—I could use your services on my jobs.”
	•	Service Provider → Trade Pro
“Hi, this is [Name]. Let’s team up—I provide services for projects like yours.”
	•	Collaborator
“Hi, let’s team up and follow the work.”

⸻

3. Access Model (simple rules)
	•	Accept = linked
	•	Assign = access
	•	No assignment = see nothing

⸻

4. Permissions by Role

Trade Pro (on client properties)
	•	Full: create work orders, notes, photos

Service Provider
	•	Only their work
	•	Can: create work orders + photos
	•	Can: add notes inside their work only
	•	Cannot: see or edit anything else

Collaborator
	•	Read only
	•	Can leave private notes (owner-facing)
	•	Cannot do work

Teammates (internal)
	•	Full access based on role

⸻

5. Structure of Relationships
	•	Homeowner / Facility = owns the property context
	•	Trade Pro = runs and organizes the work
	•	Service Provider = gets pulled in for specific tasks
	•	Collaborator = observes

⸻

6. Control Panel Mental Model
	•	You see:
	•	Your work
	•	Your people (team)
	•	Your properties
	•	Your messages

Each role just emphasizes a different one.

⸻

7. Guardrails (important edges)
	•	One pending request at a time
	•	Must accept to message
	•	Can remove/unlink anytime
	•	Removing = no future access, past work stays
	•	Providers never get automatic access to all jobs

⸻

Final Story (one line)

People request to team up → they accept → they can talk → they get assigned → they do their part of the work.

⸻

That’s now consistent top to bottom—language, permissions, and flow all aligned.
