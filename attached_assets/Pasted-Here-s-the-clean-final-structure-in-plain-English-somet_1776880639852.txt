Here’s the clean, final structure in plain English—something you can hand directly to your agent.

⸻

Reminders Page (Top to Bottom)

When the user taps Reminders, they see:
	1.	Company Reminders
Important notices from the business
	2.	Shopping List
Materials, supplies, things to buy
	3.	Active Clients
Current jobs
	4.	Top 5 (Expandable)
Priority tasks
	5.	New Lists
Custom lists the user creates
	6.	What I Need From You (Provider → Client)
This is where the contractor asks the client for things needed to move forward
	7.	Ask a Pro (Client → Provider)
This is where the client asks questions and gets answers

⸻

Two Types of Questions (Very Important)

1. Client → Provider (Gamified)

This is the Ask a Pro section.

Flow:
	•	Client asks a question
	•	Provider answers it → gets 5 points
	•	Client reviews the answer
	•	Client taps: “This answered my question”
	•	Provider gets 20 points
	•	Then client chooses what to do next:

Options:
	•	Set up an appointment
	•	Add to a list
	•	I was just curious

⸻

2. Provider → Client (No Points)

This is What I Need From You.

Purpose:
To show the client exactly what the contractor needs to continue the job.

Examples:
	•	Approve estimate
	•	Choose material
	•	Upload a photo
	•	Confirm schedule

Flow:
	•	Provider asks for something
	•	Client responds (reply, approve, upload, etc.)
	•	Once handled, it’s marked complete

No points involved.

⸻

Key Rule
	•	Only client-asked questions give points
	•	Provider-asked questions are strictly for workflow and clarity

⸻

Simple Status Logic

Client → Provider:
	•	Open
	•	Answered (waiting on client)
	•	Completed (client confirmed)

Provider → Client:
	•	Waiting on client
	•	Completed

⸻

Simple UX Labels (Use These)
	•	What I Need From You → provider asking client
	•	Ask a Pro → client asking provider
	•	Toggle text: “This answered my question”

⸻

The Idea in One Sentence

This system combines:
	•	reminders
	•	task lists
	•	and two-way communication

so both sides always know:
	•	what needs to be done
	•	what’s been answered
	•	and what the next step is
