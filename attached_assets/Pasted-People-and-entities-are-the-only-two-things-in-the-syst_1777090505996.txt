People and entities are the only two things in the system.

Entities are:
	•	Residential properties
	•	Commercial properties / facilities
	•	Businesses / companies

Entities are where all work, communication, and history happen.

People have accounts (avatars).
Accounts are identity only.

People do not connect to people.
People are added to entities.

⸻

Search

People search finds a person and shows their accounts (avatars) as context.

Company/entity search finds businesses or properties directly.

Search does not create a connection.
It starts an add to entity flow.

⸻

Core Flow

Select person → choose entity → choose how they participate → system completes invite

⸻

Step-by-step
	1.	User searches and selects a person
	2.	System asks:
“Where are you adding them?”

	•	choose a property
	•	or choose a business

	3.	System asks:
“How are you adding them here?”

Options depend on context, for example on a property:
	•	Hire through business (if they have one)
	•	Add as teammate
	•	Add as collaborator

	4.	If the person has a business, show:

	•	their business as a separate option for “hire”

	5.	User selects the option

⸻

Account (Avatar) Handling

If the person already has the needed account (avatar):
	•	create the participant record
	•	they are added to the entity

If the person does NOT have the needed account:
	•	send an account setup request tied to the invite

Example message:
“Sarah wants to add you as a teammate for Dallas Home. Set up this account to accept.”

When accepted:
	•	the system creates the required account (avatar)
	•	adds them to the entity

⸻

Important Rule

Selecting a person does not define the relationship.
The relationship is chosen explicitly during the add flow.

This prevents mistakes (e.g., hiring someone as a pro vs adding them as a family helper).

⸻

Entity Participation

All participation is stored as:
	•	entityId
	•	account (avatar)
	•	status (invited, requested, approved, etc.)
	•	permissions

All actions (messages, tasks, photos, timelines, etc.) are tied to the entity.

⸻

Client Behavior

Clients are not a separate data model.

They are a view of properties filtered by the person attached.

Example:
Search “Sheila” → opens Sheila’s property.

⸻

Final Rule

Entities hold the work.
Accounts (avatars) define the person.
All connections are created by adding people to entities.
If an account is missing, the system creates it during the invite process.
