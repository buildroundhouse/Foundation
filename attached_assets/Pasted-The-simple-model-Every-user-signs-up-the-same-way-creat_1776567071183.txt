The simple model

Every user signs up the same way:
    •    create account
    •    username
    •    required photo

Then the app asks:

What are you here as right now?
    •    Trade Pro
    •    Home
    •    Facilities
    •    Collaborator

If they tap Collaborator, then it asks:
    •    Trade Pro Collaborator
    •    Facilities Collaborator

That is the only split.

After that, each path gets a short intake that builds a profile that already feels alive before they enter it.

⸻

What each path really is

1. Trade Pro

This is the person doing the work.

Their intake should answer:
    •    what trade are you
    •    how experienced are you
    •    where do you work
    •    what kind of company is this
    •    what does your work look like

Their profile should feel like:
    •    work engine
    •    daily log
    •    timeline
    •    clients
    •    invoices
    •    estimates
    •    receipts
    •    notes

Core feeling:
I run my day here.

⸻

    •    what does it feel like visually

Their profile should feel like:
    •    warm

    •    visual

    •    goals

Core feeling:
This is my place.

The real product rule

The intake is not just setup.
The intake decides the tone, tools, and first screen.

That means:
    •    Trade Pro intake creates a work brain
    •    Home intake creates a home base
    •    Facilities intake creates a control center
    •    Collaborator intake creates a worker role

So the engine should not think:
“collect data.”

It should think:
“build the right world for this person.”

⸻

The deeper rule underneath all of it

One person may eventually have:
    •    Trade Pro
    •    Home
    •    Facilities

all under one login.

That is not edge case behavior.
That is normal behavior.

So the system is:

one account
→ multiple modes
→ each mode unlocked by passing through its own intake

That is the cleanest version of what you’ve been describing.

The shortest version to give your engine

Build onboarding as a gated identity builder.

All users create one account with username and required profile photo.
Then they choose a mode: Trade Pro, Home, Facilities, or Collaborator.
If Collaborator is selected, split into Trade Pro Collaborator or Facilities Collaborator.

Each mode has a short required intake that defines:
    •    who they are
    •    what they manage or do
    •    what their profile should feel like
    •    what tools should appear first

The purpose of intake is not just registration.
It is to generate a profile that already has identity, tone, and direction before the user enters it.
